1. What is CRM?
   CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management. It is a strategy and technology used by businesses to manage all interactions with customers and potential customers. It helps organizations stay connected to customers, streamline processes, and improve profitability.
2. Why Companies Use Salesforce
   Salesforce is a leader in CRM because it offers a unified cloud platform. Companies use it to:
   Connect with Customers: Bring marketing, sales, and service together for a 360-degree view of the customer.
   Scalability: It works for both small businesses and large enterprises.
   Efficiency: uses AI and automation to make employees more productive and understand customer needs better.
3. Core Salesforce Terminology
   Account: Represents a company or organization that you have a relationship with.
   Contact: Represents an individual person associated with an Account.
   Opportunity:Represents a potential sales deal or a "win" you are tracking in the pipeline.
4. Real-World Mapping: College Admission System
   I have chosen the College Admission scenario to map how Salesforce concepts work in a real-life environment:
   Salesforce Term | Mapping to College Admission |
   Account | The University/College (The institution) |
   Contact | The Student (The person applying) |
   Lead | An Inquirer (Someone who filled out a "Contact Us" form) |
   Opportunity| The Admission Application (Tracking if they will enroll) |
5. The Business Flow (Lead to Customer)
   In my own words, here is how a business turns a stranger into a customer:
   Lead: A person shows initial interest (e.g., clicks an ad or signs up for a newsletter).
   Contact: Once the lead is qualified, they are converted into a Contact.
   Opportunity: You identify a specific product or service they want to buy and track the deal progress.
   Customer: Once the Opportunity is "Closed Won," the individual/company is officially a customer.
